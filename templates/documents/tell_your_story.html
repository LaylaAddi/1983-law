Tell Your Story

This is a Legal Document

Your story will form the foundation of your Section 1983 civil rights complaint, which will be filed in federal court. Accuracy matters. Take your time and include as much detail as you can remember.

Prefer to speak? Use our mobile app to record your story with voice. Your audio will be transcribed and processed the same way.

What to Include:

Describe what happened in your own words. Don't worry about legal language - just tell us your story as if you're explaining it to a friend. The more detail you provide, the better we can help build your case.

  • When and where did this happen? (dates, times, addresses, city, state)
  • Who was involved? (officer names, badge numbers, agencies)
  • What was said? (quote conversations as accurately as possible)
  • What was done? (physical actions, arrests, searches)
  • What harm did you suffer? (injuries, emotional distress, financial losses)
  • Who else saw this? (witnesses, bystanders)
  • Do you have evidence? (video, photos, documents)

Tips for a Good Story
  • Be specific about dates and times - "August 8th at 1:15 PM" instead of "last week"
  • Name the people involved - Include officer names, badge numbers if you know them
  • Describe the location - Include the city, state, and specific location
  • Quote what was said - Include dialogue as accurately as you can remember
  • Describe physical actions - Did anyone push, grab, handcuff, or touch you?
  • Mention witnesses - Were there other people who saw what happened?
  • Note any evidence - Do you have video, photos, or documents?
